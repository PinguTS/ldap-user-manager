# User Guide: Getting Started

This guide is for regular users of LDAP User Manager. You do not need to know anything about LDAP or system administration to follow these steps.

## Logging In

Open the URL your administrator has provided (for example, `https://app.example.org`).

You will see a login form. Enter your **email address** and **password**, then click **Log in**.

If your organization uses single sign-on (OIDC), you may be redirected to a central login page instead of seeing a password form. Follow the on-screen instructions to log in with your existing account.

If you cannot log in, contact your administrator. Common reasons:

- Your account has not been created yet.
- Your account has been disabled.
- You entered the wrong password — use the "Forgot password" link if available.

## Changing Your Password

1. After logging in, click your name or the **Account** menu in the top navigation.
2. Click **Change password**.
3. Enter your current password, then enter and confirm your new password.
4. Click **Save**.

Your new password must meet the password strength requirements configured by your administrator. The form shows a strength indicator as you type.

If you have forgotten your password and there is a **Forgot password** link on the login page, click it and enter your email address. You will receive an email with a secure link to set a new password. The link expires after a limited time (typically 60 minutes).

## Viewing Your Profile

After logging in, you can view your own account details from the **Account** menu. Depending on your organization's configuration, you may be able to see:

- Your name and email address
- Your organization membership
- Your role (User, Organization Administrator, etc.)

You cannot edit most profile fields yourself. Contact your administrator if your information needs to be updated.

## Requesting an Account

If account requests are enabled, there will be a **Request an account** link on the login page. Click it, fill in the form with your details, and submit. Your administrator will review the request and create your account if approved.

## Logging Out

Click **Log out** in the navigation menu. Your session will be ended immediately. You will be redirected to the login page.

For security, you are automatically logged out after a period of inactivity (typically 60 minutes).

## Getting Help

If you have problems, contact your system administrator. They can:

- Reset your password.
- Re-enable your account if it has been disabled.
- Update your profile information.
- Assign you to an organization.
